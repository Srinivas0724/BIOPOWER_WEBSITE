Apartment Composting Guide for RWAs

By BIOPOWER (BPG Renewables Pvt Ltd) · Updated June 2026 · Apartments · Composting

Apartment RWAs across India are deploying OWC machines and community biogas to meet SWM Rules, cut BBMP haulage charges, and produce compost for gardens — without odour or pest issues when sized correctly.

Why apartments must act now

Municipal corporations penalise societies that dump unsegregated waste. Bengaluru, Pune, and Hyderabad actively audit bulk generators. On-site processing eliminates truck trips and builds resident pride in sustainability.

BIOPOWER supplies OWC for apartments and community biogas from 1 kg/day to 100 metric tons/day.

Step-by-step RWA rollout

  1. Baseline audit: Weigh wet waste for 7 days at the STP room.
  2. Resident awareness: Segregation posters; reject non-compostables.
  3. Equipment selection: Match peak load + 20% buffer.
  4. Civil & power: 3-phase supply, drainage for leachate.
  5. Training: Housekeeping SOPs; curing bin management.
  6. Monitoring: Monthly diversion reports for MC audits.

Cost & maintenance

100 kg/day OWC capex typically ranges ₹4–8 lakh installed. AMC covers blade sets, heaters, and microbiology kits. Compare with ongoing haulage at ₹15–25/kg — payback often under 4 years. Read apartment OWC case study.

Common mistakes

  • Undersizing for festival peaks
  • Mixing plastic and glass — kills culture
  • No curing space — wet compost smells
  • Skipping resident training

Integration with STP

Larger townships combine apartment STP with OWC — treated water for gardening, compost for landscaping. Single-vendor turnkey reduces interface risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is composting mandatory for apartments?

Under SWM Rules 2016, bulk generators including large apartments must segregate and process wet waste on-site or through authorised agencies. Many cities enforce this via RWAs.

What size OWC for 200 flats?

Assume 0.3–0.5 kg wet waste per flat/day → 60–100 kg/day. A 100–150 kg/day OWC with curing bins is typical; biogas is viable above 150 kg/day with gas use case.

How do RWAs fund composting plants?

Common models: one-time corpus, monthly maintenance fee hike (₹20–50/flats), or CSR sponsorship. Subsidy schemes may apply for community biogas.
