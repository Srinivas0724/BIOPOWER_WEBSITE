Biogas Plant Subsidy in Karnataka: Complete 2026 Guide

By BIOPOWER (BPG Renewables Pvt Ltd) · Updated June 2026 · Biogas · Karnataka

Karnataka offers multiple pathways to subsidise biogas and Bio-CNG plants — from central GOBARDhan and MNRE programmes to state energy department facilitation. Eligible hotels, apartments, and institutions can recover 20–40% of capital cost when documentation and DPRs meet scheme criteria.

Why Karnataka is prioritising biogas

Karnataka generates millions of tonnes of municipal and commercial organic waste annually. Bengaluru alone processes segregated wet waste through BBMP mandates, while tier-2 cities expand source-segregation under SWM Rules 2016. Converting this stream into biogas in Karnataka reduces landfill pressure, cuts LPG spend, and supports corporate ESG reporting.

Eligibility checklist

  • Registered entity (hotel, society, institution, or company) with GST/PAN
  • Site ownership or long-term lease with NOC from landlord
  • Documented daily organic waste quantity (minimum thresholds vary by scheme)
  • KSPCB consent where applicable for effluent or digestate discharge
  • Bankable DPR from an experienced EPC such as BIOPOWER biogas plants

Subsidy application process — step by step

  1. Waste audit: Measure feedstock type, volume, and contamination.
  2. Technology selection: Portable, institutional, or Bio-CNG upgrading based on gas offtake.
  3. DPR preparation: CAPEX, OPEX, emission reductions, and payback model.
  4. Scheme registration: Apply on the relevant portal with entity KYC.
  5. Site inspection: Third-party or department verification.
  6. Sanction & disbursement: Typically milestone-linked — advance, commissioning, performance guarantee.

How much subsidy can you expect?

Subsidy is capacity-linked. A 500 kg/day institutional plant may attract a different percentage than a 5 TPD CBG cluster. As a planning benchmark, many commercial projects in Karnataka target 25–35% effective capital recovery through combined central grant and accelerated depreciation. Actual amounts depend on the active circular and MNRE guidelines at sanction date — always verify with the latest GO.

Documents required

  • Entity registration, PAN, GST
  • Site layout and utility drawings
  • Waste generation certificate or self-declaration with photos
  • Environmental clearance / KSPCB consent if triggered
  • Quotations from MNRE-listed or scheme-recognised suppliers
  • Bank account details for subsidy credit

Common rejection reasons — and how to avoid them

Applications fail when waste data is inconsistent, technology is undersized, or digestate disposal is undefined. BIOPOWER DPRs include mass balance, biogas yield calculations, and O&M plans that align with evaluator checklists — reducing back-and-forth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a biogas subsidy in Karnataka?

Yes. Karnataka projects can access central schemes such as GOBARDhan and MNRE waste-to-energy programmes, plus state facilitation through KREDL and local urban bodies depending on project scale and feedstock.

Who is eligible for a biogas subsidy?

Hotels, apartments, dairies, institutions, and aggregators with documented organic waste streams and compliant site plans are typically eligible. Individual household portable units may qualify under separate SBM-linked programmes.

How long does subsidy approval take?

DPR submission to sanction usually takes 8–16 weeks depending on scheme, bank appraisal, and KSPCB clearance timelines. BIOPOWER assists with documentation to reduce rework.
