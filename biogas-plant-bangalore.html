How It Works

Anaerobic Digestion, Explained Simply

A biogas plant uses anaerobic digestion — bacteria breaking down organic waste in an oxygen-free digester — to release methane-rich biogas, which is captured and piped directly to a stove or generator, with the leftover slurry recovered as nutrient-rich organic manure.

1

Feed Waste

Food, kitchen & organic waste is fed into the digester daily.

2

Anaerobic Digestion

Bacteria break down waste in an oxygen-free tank over 20–30 days.

3

Biogas Generated

Methane-rich gas collects and is piped to a gas holder or stove.

4

Clean Energy Use

Gas powers cooking, water heating, or a generator on-site.

5

Digestate Output

Nutrient-rich slurry is recovered as organic liquid manure.

Benefits of a Biogas Plant for Your Bangalore Property

🔥

Renewable Cooking Energy

Replace or supplement LPG with on-site biogas generated from your own waste stream.

Waste Diversion

Cuts the volume of organic waste sent to landfills or BBMP collection, lowering disposal costs.

🌱

Nutrient-Rich Digestate

Recovered slurry doubles as organic liquid fertilizer for landscaping or kitchen gardens.

🌍

Lower Emissions

Anaerobic digestion of organic waste avoids methane release from open dumping — converting cattle manure and food waste into biogas instead of letting it decompose uncontrolled is one of the more effective ways to cut short-lived greenhouse gases.

💰

Lower Utility Bills

Commercial kitchens typically offset a meaningful share of LPG cost once the plant reaches steady state.

🛠️

Low Maintenance

No moving parts in the digester itself — routine feeding and periodic desludging keep it running for years.

FAQs

Common Questions About Biogas Plants in Bangalore

Which areas in Bangalore does BIOPOWER install biogas plants in?
We install and service biogas plants across Bangalore, including Whitefield, Electronic City, Koramangala, Indiranagar, HSR Layout, Marathahalli, Yelahanka, and the Hennur–Kacharakanahalli belt, plus nearby towns in Karnataka.
How much space does a commercial biogas plant need?
It depends on capacity. Portable plants up to 100 kg/day fit in a balcony-sized footprint, while commercial plants from 125 kg/day upward need a dedicated outdoor pad — exact dimensions are confirmed during a free site assessment.
Is government subsidy available for biogas plants in Karnataka?
Subsidy support is periodically available through MNRE's National Biogas Programme and Karnataka's state nodal agencies. Eligibility depends on plant size, applicant category, and the current scheme status — our team verifies this for every project.
How long does installation take?
Portable biogas plants can be installed within a few days of site readiness. Commercial and institutional plants typically take 4–8 weeks depending on capacity, civil work, and feedstock handling needs.
What kind of waste can the plant process?
Kitchen scraps, food waste, vegetable market refuse, and cattle or dairy manure are all suitable feedstocks — our engineers confirm the right digester sizing based on your actual waste composition and quantity.
